ABOUT US

Anxiety UK has grown from a small charity into a national organisation working to support people living with anxiety. We are specialists with years of experience of anxiety, for people with anxiety. Our small team of four is led by an amazing board of trustees who are crucial to how we operate.

In 2020, we marked our 50th anniversary with a commissioned artwork by Benami, celebrating our journey and long-standing commitment to sufferers. This is symbolised by the two individuals holding hands proudly in our boardroom as a tribute to everyone who has shaped Anxiety UK.

Trading Paralysis for Purpose

My anxiety manifested as total overwhelm and paralysis. It wasn't just fear of big things; it was the fear of everything on the list: booking a doctor's appointment, replying to a slightly critical email, or even just deciding what to wear. I would get stuck in a loop of indecision until I felt physically sick, leaving me unproductive and incredibly guilty. I knew I was smart and capable, but my own mind was my biggest saboteur. I turned to Anxiety UK primarily for their online resources and community forums, as I was too anxious initially to even talk on the phone. What they offered wasn't a magic cure, but a comprehensive toolkit. I focused heavily on their Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) modules. I learned how to identify my "thought traps"—the ways my mind catastrophized every small issue into a disaster. The program taught me to challenge those thoughts, replacing "I will fail this presentation" with "I might feel anxious, but I am prepared and I can manage." One of the biggest breakthroughs was their mindfulness training. I learned that the tightness in my chest and the ache in my jaw were physical reactions to mental noise, and that I could interrupt that cycle. Now, when the panic starts to rise—like before an important client call—I don't fight it. I do the Anxiety UK 5-4-3-2-1 grounding exercise, I remind myself of the facts, and I proceed anyway. Anxiety UK didn't make my anxiety disappear, but they gave me the map and the compass. Now, I have agency over my days. I’m not just surviving the overwhelm; I’m navigating it successfully, armed with proven techniques. The sense of empowerment I feel is indescribable.
